The Central Bank of Ireland is the national central bank and integrated financial regulator, established under the Central Bank Act 1942 and restructured by the Central Bank Reform Act 2010. It is a member of the European System of Central Banks and participates in the single supervisory mechanism of the European Central Bank, which sets monetary policy for the euro area. Domestically it supervises banks, insurers, investment firms, credit unions and other regulated entities operating in or from Ireland. The Central Bank is constituted as a statutory corporation independent of government and is funded primarily through levies on the firms it regulates rather than by direct Oireachtas grant.
